IV. Buddha as a musician in Ajataka stories

The role of music in Buddhist practice

Welcoming Descent of Amida and Bodhisattvas - See more from the MET collection.

The Buddha is often depicted as a musician in art and literature, emphasizing the importance of music in Buddhist practice. One example of this can be seen in the Ajataka story, which tells the tale of the Buddha's past lives as a musician. In this story, the Buddha is shown using his musical abilities to inspire compassion and alleviate suffering in others. This reflects the Buddhist belief in the power of music to evoke positive emotions and promote spiritual well-being.

Furthermore, the Ajataka story highlights the importance of music in Buddhist meditation practice. The story emphasizes the role of music in creating a peaceful and harmonious atmosphere, which can help practitioners to achieve a state of calm and clarity in meditation.

Sho Instrument

It is a mouth organ with a series of bamboo pipes, each with a metal reed, producing distinct sounds when air is blown into it. The sho is known for its ethereal, resonant tones that evoke a sense of spiritual depth and tranquility.
